--- layout: post title: 2015 Was Fun --- I truly loved the year 2015 - it was fun, inspiring and exciting. And this is why:

Spring

In spring I passed my final exams and got my German and French high school diploma, what felt really relieving. I also found a university of applied sciences where I'd love to study - it's great to have something to look forward to. Oh, and I had a great time at the German Scratch Wiki Day!

Summer

In summer I helped organize the Scratch2015AMS Conference. I loved the organizing meetings in Paris and Amsterdam and working with a great team of people. Scratch Conferences are my favorite part of the year and participating again, meeting many cool Scratchers and being overwhelmed by all the creative and new things I saw was just awesome!

Autumn

In autumn I had much free time since I didn't start studying yet. I used that time to relax, to create a Scratch Workshop (which was also my first paid job ever), to get more active in my local CoderDojo and to start helping out at a refugees' hostel. On the brink of winter I went to a Spanish Language School in Spain for a month, where I enjoyed the great weather and nature. It was a valuable time of experiences and getting to know myself better.

Winter

In winter I got my second paid job ever which is also the best job in the world: I joined the Scratch Team as a (remote) Community Moderator. Even after all my time with Scratch, this was still a big step for me and I still can't believe it. It was also very confusing to understand how the German law for such things works and I think I've learned a lot from dealing with that.

I want to thank everyone who was involved in making my year that awesome. It wouldn't have worked without you.

(in no particular order)

  • my (highschool and language school) teachers
  • my family who supported me when life was stressful
  • the Scratch2015AMS Organizing Committee and all participants of the Conference for all their work and for being amazing
  • the Children's and Youth's Office in my town who supported me when I suggested making a Scratch Workshop
  • my lovely Spanish host family
  • the Scratch Community, especially it's German speaking part
  • the one and only Scratch Team
